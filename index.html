What is the Low FODMAP Diet?

The Low FODMAP diet is a scientifically-backed elimination diet designed to help people with Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) and other digestive disorders identify trigger foods.

What are FODMAPs?

FODMAPs are short-chain carbohydrates that are poorly absorbed in the small intestine. They include:

  • F ermentable - broken down by gut bacteria
  • O ligosaccharides - found in wheat, onions, garlic
  • D isaccharides - lactose in dairy products
  • M onosaccharides - fructose in fruits
  • And P olyols - sugar alcohols in some fruits and artificial sweeteners

Who Should Try This Diet?

  • People with IBS (Irritable Bowel Syndrome)
  • Those with Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO)
  • Individuals with inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) in remission
  • Anyone experiencing unexplained digestive symptoms

The Three Phases

1. Elimination (2-6 weeks)

Remove all high FODMAP foods

2. Reintroduction (6-8 weeks)

Systematically test each FODMAP group

3. Maintenance (Long-term)

Eat only foods you tolerate well

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I stay on the elimination phase?

+

The elimination phase typically lasts 2-6 weeks, depending on your symptoms. Most people see improvement within 2-4 weeks. If you don't see any improvement after 6 weeks, the Low FODMAP diet may not be right for you.

Can I eat bananas on a Low FODMAP diet?

+

Yes! Ripe bananas are low FODMAP in small portions (1 medium banana). However, unripe bananas are high in FODMAPs. Stick to yellow bananas with brown spots for the best results.

Is the Low FODMAP diet safe long-term?

+

The elimination phase should not be long-term. After identifying your triggers, you should reintroduce as many foods as possible to maintain a balanced diet. Work with a dietitian for long-term guidance.

Can I drink alcohol on the Low FODMAP diet?

+

Some alcoholic beverages are low FODMAP in small amounts, including wine, beer (in moderation), and spirits. However, alcohol can irritate the gut, so it's best to limit consumption during the elimination phase.
